B. J. Habibie

The Engineer Who Lifted Indonesia to the Skies

Here is a timeline of B. J. Habibie's life:

1936

Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie was born in Parepare, South Sulawesi, Indonesia.

1955

Moved to Germany to study mechanical and aeronautical engineering.

1960

Earned his engineering degree from RWTH Aachen University.

1965

Completed his doctoral degree summa cum laude in aeronautical engineering.

1974

Returned to Indonesia and began leading the development of the national aerospace industry.

1978

Appointed Minister of Research and Technology of Indonesia.

1995

Played a key role in the development of the N-250 Gatotkaca, Indonesia’s first domestically designed turboprop aircraft.

1998

Became the 3rd President of Indonesia during a critical period of political reform.

1999

Oversaw democratic reforms, including press freedom and Indonesia’s first free general election.

2019

Passed away at the age of 83, leaving a lasting legacy in science, technology, and leadership.
